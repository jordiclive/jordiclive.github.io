# Book Recommendations

### What I liked in 2018

<span style="color:green">The Strangest Man: The Hidden Life of Paul Dirac, Mystic of the Atom</span> – by *Graham Farmelo* (**2009**)

<a href="https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/6629359-the-strangest-man">
  <img src="https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/I/81U74hT9fFL.jpg" alt="Dirac" height= "236" width="150" style="float: left;margin:0 10px 10px 0" />
</a>

I found this the most remarkable page-turner. Partly because Farmelo is obsessed, he is an assiduous, observant and fair biographer. 
The book reveals the fragility of the man, along with the awesome power of the young Paul Dirac: Principles The Principles of Quantum Mechanics, is still a touted text today, due to Dirac’s inspired clarity. Dirac’s humble origins were completely atypical, especially compared to the quantum avant-garde; this book describes how the quiet man ended up with the most incredible life, becoming enmeshed in all the great historical events of that period. Dirac set off the chain-reaction of physicists predicting particles through thought alone

Conversely, I did not find him altogether that strange, Henry Cavendish or Nikola Tesla were indeed stranger; however, his actual love letters to his wife, Manci (Margit Wigner), in their courtship are a highlight.
